Frequently Asked Questions(FAQs)
  • Q1. What is RecipeDB?
    RecipeDB is a repository of structured data of over 100,000 recipes and approximately 2000 ingredients across over 22 world regions, intended to enable data-driven explorations of recipes. In conjunction with data of flavor molecules from FlavorDB, RecipeDB facilitates multi-level analysis of traditional recipes (recipes, categorical classifications, ingredient composition).
  • Q2. What are the different entities represented in RecipeDB and how do they relate to each other?
    Each recipe comprises of an ‘ingredients section’ and an ‘instructions section’. In the ingredients section, each ingredient is associated with a certain quantity, unit of measurement, state, temperature and process. In the instructions section, each ingredient is associated to a process. The instructions section also contains the utensils used. Furthermore, each ingredient is associated with a category.
  • Q3. What types of queries are processed by RecipeDB?
    RecipeDB facilitates an elastic search to query recipes based on factors such as geographical location of origin (i.e. continent, region, country), categories and ingredients associated/unassociated with the recipes and nutritional information (i.e. calorie, protein, etc. content).

  • Q6. What are the various recipes within RecipeDB?
    A list of all the recipes in RecipeDB can be obtained by simply hitting the ‘Search’ button on the recipe search page without providing any specific query.
  • Q7. What are RecipeDB Statistics?
    RecipeDB Statistics explain the vastness and depth of RecipeDB using interactive Google Charts.
  • Q8. What are the prerequisites required in order to use RecipeDB?
    A modern web browser.
  • Q9. What browsers are supported by RecipeDB?
    RecipeDB supports all modern web browsers, but it can be best viewed on Chrome, Firefox and Edge.
  • Q10. What is the Tech Stack used to build RecipeDB?
    Frontend: Bootstrap, Materialize CSS, JavaScript, Google Charts Backend: Flask, SQLite, Python
  • Q11. What are the sources of information for RecipeDB?
    RecipeDB has sourced recipes from Genius Kitchen, allrecipes, and Tarla Dalal. RecipeDB has sourced nutritional information from USDA databases.
  • Q12. Does RecipeDB use cookies?
    RecipeDB uses cookies to provide statistics that help us give the best possible experience for our site.
  • Q13. Can I access RecipeDB using my mobile phone?
    Although RecipeDB is best viewed on a desktop, the website is accessible from most modern mobile web browsers.
